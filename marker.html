AR Marker — Operando PEFC Visualiser

Print and affix these elements to your Gordon Conference poster. Visitors scan with their phone to see the 3D fuel cell model in augmented reality.

AR Marker (Hiro pattern)

Print at exactly 8×8 cm for reliable detection. The white border is part of the marker — do not trim it. Affix to poster in the Conclusions or blank lower region.

Print instructions: Print at 100% scale (no "fit to page"). Target 8×8 cm. Flat, matte paper preferred — glossy causes reflections. Keep the white border intact.

QR Code Generator

Enter the URL where you host the AR experience (your GitHub Pages link). The QR code is printed alongside the Hiro marker.

QR will appear here

Print QR at 4×4 cm minimum. Place immediately next to or below the Hiro marker.

Setup instructions


1

Upload the AR experience to GitHub Pages (or any static host). Your repo URL becomes: https://USERNAME.github.io/REPO/

2

Paste your URL above and click Generate QR. Download both the QR code and Hiro marker.

3

Print the Hiro marker at 8×8 cm and QR code at 4×4 cm. Laminate or use gloss-free paper for best tracking.

4

Affix both to your poster — the marker in a visible unobstructed area (lower-left or lower-right). Add a small label: "Scan QR then point camera at marker to view 3D model"

5

Visitors open the URL (or scan QR), choose a model, tap AR Camera, and point their phone camera at the marker. The 3D fuel cell appears floating above the poster.

Suggested poster annotation

Add this text near the marker on your poster:
"Scan to explore the 3D operando PEFC — rotate, explode components, and examine neutron beam paths in augmented reality."
